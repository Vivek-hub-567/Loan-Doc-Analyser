SARFAESI Act — Securitisation and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act, 2002

KEY PROVISIONS FOR BORROWERS

1. SECURED CREDITOR RIGHTS
Banks and NBFCs can enforce security interest without court intervention.
Applicable only when NPA (Non-Performing Asset) classification occurs after 3+ months of default.

2. MANDATORY NOTICE REQUIREMENT
60-day notice must be given to borrower before possession of secured asset.
Borrower has right to repay dues and reclaim asset within the notice period.
Notice must be in writing and specify the amount due and nature of default.

3. APPEAL RIGHTS
Borrower can appeal to the Debt Recovery Tribunal (DRT) within 45 days of receiving notice.
DRT can grant stay on asset possession if substantial cause is shown.
Appeal to Debt Recovery Appellate Tribunal (DRAT) lies from DRT orders.

4. MINIMUM LOAN THRESHOLD
SARFAESI applies only to loans above ₹1 lakh.
Agricultural land is exempt from SARFAESI proceedings.

5. BORROWER PROTECTIONS
Fair valuation of asset must be conducted before sale.
Borrower has right to receive surplus proceeds after debt recovery.
Public notice of sale must be given with minimum 30 days notice.

6. COMMON ILLEGAL PRACTICES TO WATCH
Immediate seizure without 60-day notice is illegal.
Recovery agent harassment or coercion violates RBI guidelines and may be criminal.
Seizing household goods not pledged as security is illegal.
