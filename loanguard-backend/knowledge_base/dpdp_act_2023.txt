DPDP Act 2023 — Digital Personal Data Protection Act, India

KEY PROVISIONS RELEVANT TO DIGITAL LENDING

1. CONSENT REQUIREMENTS
Data fiduciaries (lenders/apps) must obtain free, specific, informed, unconditional, and unambiguous consent.
Blanket consent to all data collection is NOT valid under the DPDP Act.
Consent must be obtained through a clear, plain-language notice.
Borrowers have the right to withdraw consent at any time.

2. PURPOSE LIMITATION
Personal data may only be collected for specific, explicit, and legitimate purposes.
Digital lenders cannot collect contact lists, SMS data, or location for purposes beyond credit assessment.
Data cannot be retained beyond the period necessary for the stated purpose.

3. DATA PRINCIPAL RIGHTS
Right to access personal data held by the data fiduciary.
Right to correction and erasure of inaccurate or no-longer-needed data.
Right to grievance redressal against data fiduciaries.
Right to nominate a representative for data decisions in case of death/incapacity.

4. DATA FIDUCIARY OBLIGATIONS
Must implement reasonable security safeguards against data breaches.
Must notify borrowers in case of a data breach affecting their personal data.
Cannot transfer personal data to countries not approved by the Government of India.
Must appoint a Data Protection Officer for significant data fiduciaries.

5. CHILDREN'S DATA
Digital lenders must not collect data from borrowers who are minors.
Verifiable parental consent is required for processing data of minors.

6. PENALTIES
Non-compliance with DPDP Act can result in penalties up to ₹250 crore per violation.
The Data Protection Board of India has adjudicatory powers.

7. SENSITIVE DATA IN LENDING CONTEXT
Financial data, identity data, and behavioral data collected by lending apps are personal data.
Medical data, precise location data, and biometric data are sensitive personal data requiring higher protection.
