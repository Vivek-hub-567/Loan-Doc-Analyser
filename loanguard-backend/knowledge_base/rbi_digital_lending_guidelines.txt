RBI Digital Lending Guidelines — Key Provisions for Borrowers

MASTER DIRECTION — DIGITAL LENDING (RBI, 2022, Updated 2023)

1. DIRECT DISBURSEMENT REQUIREMENT
All loan disbursements must be made directly to the borrower's bank account.
No loan funds may be routed through the Lending Service Provider (LSP) or Digital Lending App (DLA).
Repayments must be made directly to the Regulated Entity (NBFC/Bank), not to the LSP.

2. KEY FACT STATEMENT (KFS)
Every digital lender must provide a standardized Key Fact Statement before loan execution.
The KFS must include:
- Annual Percentage Rate (APR) — all-inclusive cost of credit
- Processing fees, penalties, and all other charges in absolute rupee terms
- Cooling-off period details
- Grievance redressal officer contact information

3. COOLING-OFF PERIOD
Borrowers must be given a minimum cooling-off period (stipulated by RBI) to exit the loan.
No prepayment penalty may be charged if the borrower exits during the cooling-off period.

4. GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL
Every NBFC/Bank must designate a Nodal Grievance Redressal Officer.
Contact details must be displayed on the app and in all loan documents.
Borrowers may escalate to the RBI Integrated Ombudsman Scheme if unresolved within 30 days.

5. FAIR PRACTICES CODE
Lenders must follow a Board-approved Fair Practices Code.
Collection agents must carry authorization letters and not use coercive methods.
Recovery must not involve harassment, abuse, or contact with unauthorized persons.

6. DATA COLLECTION RESTRICTIONS
Digital Lending Apps may only collect data necessary for credit assessment.
Access to contacts, SMS, files, and other sensitive data requires explicit, revocable consent.
Data sharing with third parties requires specific borrower consent — blanket consent is not valid.
Lenders must comply with the Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act, 2023.

7. NBFC REGISTRATION
All digital lending entities must be registered with RBI as NBFCs or partner with registered NBFCs.
Unregistered entities offering credit through digital platforms are illegal under RBI regulations.

8. INTEREST RATE DISCLOSURE
The effective annual interest rate must be disclosed in percentage per annum terms.
Lenders must disclose the basis for floating rate changes (MCLR, repo rate, etc.).
